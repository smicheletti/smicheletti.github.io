Energy Saving on Campus

Adopting energy saving behaviors into one's life can be a difficult task - you know you should but it's hard to break habits. UT already has taken great steps to amp up energy efficiency, but a lackluster student population is difficult to excite when it comes to being green - until Electrocuties. Playing off of Millennial's love of childhood games such as Pokemon and Digimon, the gameplay allows for players to have fun while creating beneficial habits.

This project was a team effort and collaborated on by myself, Daniel Shipley (UX/Research), Savannah Hicks (UI/Illustration/Research/Branding), and Peter Silkowski (Research/Branding/Proposal). You can view their work and portfolios by clicking on their names above.

Mobile Gameplay

Electrocuties as a mobile game incentivizes positive energy-saving behavior on UT's campus by students. Through interactions with a customizable cutie that users create, simple tasks are incentivized through a rewards system encouraging users to integrate these minimal changes into their daily lives and habits. After downloading the game, the user is prompted to customize and personalize a cutie that they use to play throughout the game. Leaderboards and social interactions encourage users to participate in behaviors in order to gain high scores and compete with friends.

Spaces on Campus

Sensors installed on campus connect to the user's phone through blue-tooth in order to record whenever students participate in energy saving behaviors. These behaviors serve to unlock mini games through which players can gain items and accessories for their cuties. Each behavior is a minor change from students' daily routines that creates impact on campus physically as well as inside the game.
