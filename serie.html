How can we keep The Serie Project alive?

The Serie Project

The new website will be live at: serieproject.com after October 1st, 2015.

Sam Coronado's intention for the Serie Project was to create a solid platform that allowed for both established and emerging Latino artists to reach a bigger audience, and in turn, empower their words and creativity to spread. Since his passing in 2013, the Serie Project has continued to keep alive his vision through advocating awareness of cultural diversity in the Austin art community through advancing the fine art of serigraphy.

The Serie Project was recently awarded grant money by the City of Austin to rennovate the studio space into a combibned gallery and community serigraphy learning center. In order to give back to the community, we hope to design a welcoming space to help share Sam's love of art and serigraphy with the surrounding community. The entire studio space will be redone and circulum established for community art classes.

Revitalizing the Project

In order to continue being a presence, the Serie Project has begun to undergo a revitalization. With a multitude of prints on hand, Sam's wife Jill Ramirez hopes to sell them to institutions and collectors across the country in order to fund the redo of the studio space (for updates view Serie Gallery Space WIP). In order to do this, I am in the process of migrating their website from their outdated WordPress to a new SquareSpace site. The Serie Project's WordPress website was in store for some much needed design changes to make it more user friendly, mobile optimization, as well as to better suit it for its new main purpose: selling and distributing the serigraph prints. Since I won't always be around to make changes or manage orders, I chose SquareSpace because of its great user interface which will make it much easier for Jill and her co-worker Linda to manage in the future.
