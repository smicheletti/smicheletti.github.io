How can we provide a unique perspective on Austin?

Fall 2013

There have been songs, movies, stories, and photographs highlighting Austin, TX. However, as young creators we see and experience the city differently. As Publisher & Manager, I lead our University of Texas Design Class of 2015 in an effort to create a publication that explores subcultures and supercultures of Austin through our intimate one-on-one experiences. This book contains text, photography, paintings, graphic design, and video in order to highlight 18 unique perspectives on Austin.

For the publication, we ran a successful Indiegogo campaign, were featured in a Daily Texan article, and received the 2014 Core77 Design Award Student Runner Up in the Visual Communication Category.

*If interested in purchasing a book, feel free to contact me.

My Section

My section "Red State, Blue City" delved into the interplay between the extreme conservative and liberal groups that can be found coexisting here in Austin. While Texas is a conservative state, Austin exists as an island of liberalism. Through a combination of facts and opinions, objective and subjective criticism, as well as serious and comical tones, hopefully “Red State, Blue City” is an exploration of these two opposing views that will lead you to a better understanding of each.

Publication

Our final published book is 288 pages, 6 x 9 inches, and CMYK full color, containing our unique perspectives divided into 18 sections. The sections are divided up by each designer with each being 12-20 pages.

Fundraising

As publisher and project manager, I lead the fundraising effort in order to publish the book with our desired specifications. We ran a successful Indiegogo campaign and ultimately raised $5,700 in 27 days, 114% of our original goal! The perks ranged from personalized invitations to our launch party to hand letterpressed bookmark sets to the actual publication itself.
